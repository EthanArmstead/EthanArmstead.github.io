Treatment for the Illness

Tuberculosis disease can be treated by taking several drugs for 6 to 9 months. There are 10 drugs currently approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) for treating TB. Of the accepted drugs, the first-line of anti-TB agents that form the primary course of drugs are: Isoniazid, Rifampin, Ethambutol, and Pyrazinamide. TBfacts.org states that: “For new patients with presumed drug susceptible pulmonary TB, the World Health Organisation (WHO) recommends that they should have six months of TB treatment. This consists of a two month intensive TB treatment phase followed by a four month continuation phase.” The site also explains that TB cases can indeed be cured with the use antibiotics.
