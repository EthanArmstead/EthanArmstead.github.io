What is Tuberculosis?

Tuberculosis is a severe ariborne disease that is caused by an infection with Mycobacterium tuberculosis (MTB) bacteria. TB mainly affects the lungs but can affect any other organs of the body. It can be extremely fatal in overcrowded and low income communities and is one of the World's leading causes of death.


Some Symptoms:

  • Chronic or persistent cough and sputum production, if severe enough sputum with blood may be produced
  • Fatigue
  • Lack of Appetite
  • Weight Loss
  • Fever
  • Night Sweats

Treatment

Tuberculosis disease can be treated by taking several drugs for 6 to 9 months. There are 10 drugs currently approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) for treating TB. Although futher treatment is likely required for drug resistent strands of tuberculosis.


Susceptablility

There are reported around 6-10 million new TB cases per year. Many areas of severe affect are Eastern Europe, South Africa, and East Asia. While the illness does affect people in the U.S., it only has an infection rate of 2.9 per 100,000 persons as of 2016.
